"I'm a rehashed product made as a quick cash grab by a company that went out of business a month after making me."

"So... I do have to ask you... where'd you find this piece of junk?" A portly man sits at the front counter of a dimly lit repair bay. He chuckles to himself gently. He seems advanced in age.

"Well, it seems like this unit just... gave up. Either that or there was something very wrong it's interface because it was just in the middle of the charging bay that's currently being rebuilt, the one the west side. Quite an eerie sight really..." A nervous looking man stands across the counter. He is wearing a reflective orange vest with a patch labeling him as an "NCRS1 Worker."

The portly man shifts his glasses. A name tag reads "Jonas Bierfelt." "Do you think..?" He looks nervously at the worker. The worker gives a reassuring reply. "No. It was an GA2 who reported spotting it. I'd hope they'd check if it was Cavaric3, but who knows... I'd expect another GA at your door soon anyhows. It doesn't look like any model of anything --- sapient or otherwise mind you --- I've seen working on this station." The worker takes a second, thinking. "And from working over at Evanesce..." He continues, stalling for time as he lets his brain work. "No, I don't think I've seen anything like this at all."

He peers back into the repair bay; his eyelids squeeze as they try to make out the figure laying uncomfortably on the floor. His face strains as he's taken aback by its twitching. "I'm not sure why... but don't you feel like there's something wrong with it? It's like seeing a rabid4 animal... so helpless lying there on the floor..." The man's face is full of pity, something that causes the repair worker to swivel his chair to face the unit. His face doesn't match that of the worker.

"Well yeah something's wrong with it, there's something wrong with everything you bring in," he remarks in a lighthearted tone. His eyes study the twitching figure for a moment more. "But I do agree, something is definitely different about it... Maybe it's just the missing model information that has me..." He knows this not to be the case, there's something deeper, more primal at the heart of his concerns. It's a feeling almost maternal. "I'll see what I can do." He speaks now in a reserved tone, though he's halfway to getting off his chair when he remembers he must address his client. He turns slowly. "I'd figure it's some sort of rehashed product." He says, despite not recognising any element of the design. He continues, "made as a quick cash grab by a company that went out of business a month after making it." A soft hum of a fan system starts up, then sputters to a stop. It's interposed by a beep. Neither of the men notice.

The worker nods, finally looking away from the spasming device. "As always," he remarks, trying to lighten the tone, "I hope I don't have to see you again, but by now you know that I'll be around the tavern for drinks. Hope I haven't bothered you too much this thursday5!" He chuckles slightly, but can't shake the unnerving feeling of the clinking in the darkened bay. "Ah, well you've certainly given me some work. I might be a bit late to the tavern. This thing's just too intriguing to me..." He thinks for a moment, accidentally allowing the worker to stew in his nerves a bit longer. "Ah, sorry, see you soon yeah?" The worker nods. "Sure thing, see ya Jonas." Jonas does not return the farewell as the worker walks off, possibly content with his goodbye already, but more possibly interested more in the alien object now in his possession more than the tavern or the worker.
